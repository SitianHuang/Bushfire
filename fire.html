Bushfires Caused by Accident, Burning off and Lightning in Victoria

The 2019-2020 Victoria bushfire season was catastrophic in the scale of damage caused to agricultural resources, property, infrastructure, and ecological systems.

Cause of Bushfires and Distance to the Nearest CFA Station in Victoria

From the map we can see that lightning was the main cause of Victorian bushfires, with accident and burning off followed. Dry lightning represents the main source of natural ignition, and fires that start in remote areas far away from CFA stations are difficult to access and monitor, where would be easier to caught fire.

Effect of Temperature, Solar Exposure and Wind Speed on Cause of Bushfires

This plot shows the main weather and climate characteristics of this disaster in terms of the ralationship of 3 significant factors. Clearly most of the bushfires were caught in high-level solar exposure and relatively hot days, while the wind speed seems to show random clustering and have less influence.
